TWR-CLOUD-Bern

Two-Way-Ranging-based Cooperative Localization
with an Open UWB Dataset
from Bern-University

Developed by the Communication and Distributed Systems
research group at the University of Bern

TWR-CLOUD-Bern is the largest open-access, standardized dataset to date
in terms of cooperative nodes performing cross-ranging,
designed for researchers in wireless sensor networks (WSN),
collaborative localization, and ultra-wideband (UWB) positioning.
It enables fair and reproducible evaluations of localization methods
by providing a comprehensive dataset and evaluation framework
that captures real-world environments.

TWR-Cloud-Bern Dataset at a Glance

A snapshot with key details about the experimental setup, data collection process, and dataset structure.

  • Environment

    500mÂ² of indoor space at the institute of Computer science (Engehaldenstrasse 8).

  • Nodes

    40 Raspberry Pi Zero 2W hosting a Qorvo DWM3001CDK UWB module (IEEE 802.15.4z compliant).

  • Measurements

    Time-of-Flight (ToF) measurements and reception statistics between poller and responder nodes.

  • Scans

    12 scan cycles conducted with random node placement, comprising 11 training and 1 evaluation scan.

  • Ground truth

    3D coordinates recorded using a Leica BLK360 Imaging Laser Scanner.

  • Data Structure

    Organized into pandas DataFrames and offered in pickle format for direct use in Python.
